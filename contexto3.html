Help Superlative

There are two main ways to form comparatives in English:

  • Simmer: to cook food in liquid at a low temperature, such as a soup or stew.

  • Season: to add flavorings or spices to food, such as salt, pepper, or herbs.

  • Grind: to crush or pulverize food into a fine texture, such as grinding coffee beans or spices.
  • Boil: to cook food in liquid at a high temperature, such as pasta or potatoes.

  • Beat (to beat): to mix or whip food until it becomes smooth and creamy, such as beating eggs or cream.
